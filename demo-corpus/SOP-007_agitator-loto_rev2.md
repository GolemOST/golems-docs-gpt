# SOP-007 — Slurry Tank Agitator Lockout / Tagout (LOTO)
Revision: 2 | Status: CURRENT | Owner: Maintenance Department
SYNTHETIC DEMO DOCUMENT — generic refinery, not any real plant.

## 1. Purpose
Defines the energy isolation steps required before any work on slurry tank
agitators, including gearbox, coupling, shaft, and impeller work.

## 2. Personal Locks — Current Rule (changed in Rev 2)
2.1 EVERY person working on the agitator must fit their OWN personal lock
    and personal tag to the group lockbox. One lock per person, no
    exceptions.
2.2 A supervisor's lock alone is NOT sufficient and is prohibited as the
    only means of isolation. (This supersedes the Rev 1 rule that allowed
    a single supervisor lock for short-duration work.)
2.3 Personal locks are removed only by the lock owner, in person, after
    their own work is complete.

## 3. Isolation Steps
3.1 Stop the agitator from the control room and confirm zero speed on the
    local indicator.
3.2 Open, lock, and tag the motor isolator at the local switchroom (rack
    the breaker out where the design allows).
3.3 Press the local start button for at least 5 seconds to prove
    de-energisation (try-start test). The agitator must not move.
3.4 If the tank will be entered, the agitator isolation must ALSO be
    registered on the Confined Space Entry Permit per SOP-014 section 6.2.

## 4. Stored Energy
4.1 Allow a minimum of 15 minutes after stopping for the slurry to stop
    swirling before opening any inspection hatch.
4.2 Chock or pin the impeller if working directly on shaft or coupling —
    settled slurry can rotate the impeller as it compacts.

## 5. Restoration
5.1 Confirm all persons and tools are clear of the tank and guards are
    refitted.
5.2 Each worker removes their own lock. The isolation officer removes the
    group lock LAST.
5.3 Close the permit before the agitator is re-energised.
