# SOP-001 — Centrifugal Slurry Pump Startup and Shutdown
Revision: 3 | Status: CURRENT | Owner: Operations Training Cell
SYNTHETIC DEMO DOCUMENT — generic refinery, not any real plant.

## 1. Purpose
This procedure defines the safe startup and shutdown of horizontal centrifugal
slurry pumps in continuous process service. It applies to all field operators
and shift supervisors on rotating shift.

## 2. Pre-Start Checks
2.1 Confirm the work permit is closed and all maintenance locks are removed
    from the local isolator.
2.2 Verify suction valve is FULLY OPEN and discharge valve is CLOSED before
    starting. Starting against an open discharge valve causes motor overload.
2.3 Confirm gland seal water is flowing. Minimum seal water pressure is
    350 kPa, and it must be at least 100 kPa above pump discharge pressure.
2.4 Check oil level in the bearing housing is between the MIN and MAX marks
    on the sight glass. Top up only with ISO VG 68 mineral oil.
2.5 Rotate the shaft by hand one full turn to confirm the impeller is free.
    If the shaft does not rotate freely, do NOT start the pump — raise a
    defect notification and inform the shift supervisor.

## 3. Startup
3.1 Prime the pump: vent the casing at the top vent plug until a steady
    stream of slurry-free liquid appears. Never run the pump dry — dry
    running destroys the mechanical seal in under 30 seconds.
3.2 Start the motor from the local control station.
3.3 Slowly open the discharge valve over 30 to 60 seconds while watching
    discharge pressure and motor amps.
3.4 Confirm running current is below the motor nameplate full-load amps.
3.5 Within the first 15 minutes of operation, check:
    - Bearing housing temperature: must not exceed 80 degrees Celsius.
    - No abnormal noise or vibration at the coupling guard.
    - No visible gland leakage beyond a slow drip (approximately 1 drop
      per second is acceptable for packed glands).

## 4. Normal Shutdown
4.1 Close the discharge valve slowly to minimum flow.
4.2 Stop the motor from the local control station.
4.3 Close the suction valve.
4.4 Leave gland seal water ON for 10 minutes after stopping to flush the
    seal, then close the seal water valve.
4.5 Record the shutdown, run hours, and any abnormal observations in the
    shift log before the end of shift.

## 5. Emergency Shutdown
5.1 Hit the local emergency stop if there is smoke, fire, loud grinding, or
    a gland failure spraying slurry.
5.2 Isolate seal water only AFTER the pump has stopped.
5.3 Report every emergency stop to the shift supervisor immediately and log
    it as a critical defect.

## 6. Alarm Limits Reference
| Parameter | Alarm | Trip |
|---|---|---|
| Bearing temperature | 75 C | 90 C |
| Vibration (velocity) | 7.1 mm/s | 11.2 mm/s |
| Seal water pressure low | 300 kPa | 250 kPa |
