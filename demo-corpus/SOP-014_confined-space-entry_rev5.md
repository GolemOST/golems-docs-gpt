# SOP-014 — Confined Space Entry
Revision: 5 | Status: CURRENT | Owner: HSE Department
SYNTHETIC DEMO DOCUMENT — generic refinery, not any real plant.

## 1. Purpose
Defines the mandatory controls for entry into any confined space: tanks,
digesters, sumps, ducting, and any vessel with restricted entry or exit and
the potential for a hazardous atmosphere.

## 2. Permit Requirements
2.1 No person may enter a confined space without a valid Confined Space
    Entry Permit signed by the Area Authority AND the Entry Supervisor.
2.2 The permit is valid for ONE shift only. A new shift requires permit
    revalidation, including a fresh gas test.
2.3 The permit must list every entrant by name and badge number.

## 3. Atmospheric Testing
3.1 A competent Authorised Gas Tester must test the atmosphere before entry
    and after any break in occupation longer than 30 minutes.
3.2 Acceptable entry limits:
    - Oxygen: 19.5 percent to 23.5 percent by volume.
    - Flammable gas: below 5 percent of the Lower Explosive Limit (LEL).
    - Hydrogen sulphide (H2S): below 5 ppm.
    - Carbon monoxide (CO): below 25 ppm.
3.3 Continuous gas monitoring is required inside the space whenever hot work
    is performed, or when conditions can change (adjacent lines, sludge
    disturbance, temperature rise).
3.4 If any reading moves outside the limits in 3.2, all entrants must exit
    immediately and the permit is suspended.

## 4. Standby Attendant (Hole Watch)
4.1 A dedicated attendant must remain at the entry point for the entire
    duration of the entry. The attendant must never leave the point of entry
    while anyone is inside, and must never enter the space themselves —
    even in an emergency.
4.2 The attendant maintains a live entry log of who is inside at all times.
4.3 The attendant must have a working radio and know the emergency call
    channel for the area.

## 5. Rescue
5.1 A rescue plan must be documented on the permit before first entry.
5.2 Rescue equipment (tripod, harness, retrieval winch, SCBA set) must be
    staged at the entry point — not in a store room.
5.3 The attendant raises the alarm and starts retrieval from OUTSIDE the
    space. Entry rescue is performed only by the trained rescue team.

## 6. Isolation
6.1 All process lines into the space must be positively isolated: blinded,
    physically disconnected, or double-block-and-bleed with the bleed open
    and locked.
6.2 All agitators, mixers, and rotating equipment inside the space must be
    locked out and tagged out per SOP-007 before entry.
